Texas Cottage Food Registration Guide 2025

Overview:
- Allows qualified home food producers to sell low-risk items direct to consumers.
- Requires annual registration with the Department of State Health Services.
- Must display signage noting the kitchen is not inspected by the health department.

Key Deadlines:
- Renewal deadline: June 15 each year.
- Application processing time: 10-15 business days.

Operational Requirements:
- Limit sales to non-potentially hazardous foods (baked goods, jams, dried herbs).
- Mandatory food-handler training certificate within 30 days of approval.
- Sales permitted at farmers markets, direct delivery, and online pre-orders with local pickup.
- Maintain records of production batches and customer complaints for 90 days.

Labeling Checklist:
- Product name
- Producer name and address
- Ingredient list by weight
- Allergen statement compliant with FALCPA
- Net weight in U.S. customary units
- “This product is made in a home kitchen not inspected by the Department of State Health Services.”

Inspection & Enforcement:
- Complaints trigger inspection; violations can result in fines up to $500 per day.
- Repeat violations can suspend registration for 12 months.
